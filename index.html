About

My name is Matthew Hagan, I study Quantum Computing as a Ph.D student at the University of Toronto with Nathan Wiebe. My research has focused mainly on simulating quantum systems on quantum computers, with some side projects in quantum information theory and error correction.

Papers

Below are the papers I have contributed to in reverse chronological order.

  1. Interpreting Multipartite Entanglement through Topological Summaries

    This paper explores how homological quantities derived from entanglement metrics can be used to classify ground states of error correcting codes and graph states.

  2. The Thermodynamic Cost of Ignorance

    This paper expands upon previous models of thermalization used to prepare quantum thermal states to work for arbitrary systems and temperatures.

  3. Composite qdrift-product formulas for quantum and classical simulations in real and imaginary time

    This paper extends previously developed composite or partially randomized simulation methods to imaginary time. This makes it a useful algorithm for classical Monte Carlo algorithms, which are traditionally based on Trotter-Suzuki formulas.

  4. Composite Quantum Simulations

    This paper combined randomized simulation techniques (QDrift) with deterministic techniques (Trotter formulas) to reduce simulation costs for certain classes of Hamiltonians.

Software

I've been working on learning how to write software since I first tried to develop ROMs for my slow ass android phone when I was a teen. This section is a great example that time spent doing something is not the only important metric and the quality of time spent practicing is just as important; as I only have the following packages that are worth sharing with others. All libraries are in rust unless specified otherwise.

  • Trotter-Qdrift-Simulation - A library developed alongside my collaborator Matthew Pocrnic for benchmarking composite / partially randomized quantum simulations.
  • (MHGL) Matt's HyperGraph Library - A library for working with undirected hypergraphs that can compute boundaries, links, stars, and skeletons. Working on adding homology algorithms and a simplex-tree datastructure.
  • SPARFF - Work in progress sparse linear algebra library over finite fields.
  • HDX_CODEC - Library developed to explore the error correcting codes developed in New Codes on High Dimensional Expanders and possible quantum counterparts.
